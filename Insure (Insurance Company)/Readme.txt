Online Insurance Service Web Application
Introduction

Insurance is a contract for payment of a sum of money to the person assured on the happening of the event insured against. Typically, the contract provides for payment of an amount on the date of maturity or at specified periodic intervals, or at unfortunate death if it occurs earlier. Insurance is universally acknowledged as an institution that eliminates risk by substituting certainty for uncertainty and comes to the timely aid of the family in the unfortunate event of death of the breadwinner.

Project Overview

This software offers four types of Insurance services, which include:

Life Insurance

Medical Insurance

Motor Insurance

Home Insurance

Project Features

The application is designed to be used via the Internet, allowing officials of the insurance company to view and update information online. Key features include:

Users can view details of various insurance policies and schemes offered by the company.

New users can register on the website to get online access to insurance information.

Existing policyholders can view their policy details and calculate premiums.

The website provides up-to-date information about new strategies and subsidiary schemes of the company.

Provides loan facilities for policyholders and supports online payments.

Insurance Types Explained

Life Insurance: Provides financial protection to the insured and their family in case of mishaps. Policyholders pay monthly premiums for a defined period to avail benefits.

Medical Insurance: Covers medical expenses like doctor fees, hospital bills, medicines, and other related costs due to illness or accidents.

Motor Insurance: Protects the automobile owned by the user against damage or loss.

Home Insurance: Compensates for mishaps related to the insured's home. Coverage varies depending on the policy and premiums paid. Multiple types of home insurance plans are available to suit individual needs.

Premium Calculation

The premium is calculated based on the term plan selected by the policyholder.
